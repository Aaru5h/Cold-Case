CRIME SCENE REPORT - CASE #CC-1987-042
Date: October 15, 1987
Location: 742 Maple Street, Riverside
Investigating Officer: Detective Frank Morrison

INITIAL FINDINGS:
At approximately 10:45 PM, officers responded to a 911 call reporting a disturbance at the residence. Upon arrival, the body of Richard Thornton (age 52) was discovered in the study on the ground floor.

VICTIM DESCRIPTION:
Richard Thornton, male, Caucasian, 52 years old, CEO of Thornton Industries. The victim was found slumped over his mahogany desk facing the window. A spilled glass of whiskey was noted on the desk surface.

PHYSICAL EVIDENCE:
1. A broken window on the east side of the study
2. Muddy footprints leading from the window to the desk
3. An antique letter opener with traces of blood found under the desk
4. A torn envelope with the name "Eleanor" partially visible
5. The victim's safe was open but appeared untouched

WITNESSES ON SCENE:
- Eleanor Thornton (wife) - Found the body, claims she was in the garden
- James Thornton (son, age 28) - Was out of town, arrived later
- Margaret Hayes (housekeeper) - Was in the kitchen at the time

TIME OF DEATH (preliminary): Between 9:30 PM and 10:30 PM

WEATHER CONDITIONS: Heavy rain throughout the evening

NOTES:
The victim appeared to have been in a heated argument earlier that day according to the housekeeper. Richard Thornton had recently changed his will, according to the family attorney contacted the following day.
