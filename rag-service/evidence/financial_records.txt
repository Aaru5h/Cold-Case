FINANCIAL RECORDS ANALYSIS
Case #CC-1987-042
Prepared by: Forensic Accountant David Chen
Date: October 25, 1987

SUBJECT: Richard Thornton / Thornton Industries

SUMMARY OF FINDINGS:

1. RECENT WILL CHANGES
On October 10, 1987 (five days before the murder), Richard Thornton met with attorney Harold Finch to modify his last will and testament. The changes included:
- Reducing James Thornton's inheritance from 50% to 5% of the estate
- Increasing charitable donations from 10% to 40%
- Eleanor Thornton's share remained unchanged at 45%
- A new trust was established for an unnamed beneficiary

ESTATE VALUE: Approximately $4.2 million in assets, including the Maple Street residence, Thornton Industries shares, and investment portfolios.

2. THORNTON INDUSTRIES FINANCIAL STATUS
The company has been experiencing financial difficulties:
- Revenue down 23% year-over-year
- Three major contracts lost to competitors in Q3 1987
- Rumors of potential hostile takeover by Marcus Webb of Webb Holdings

SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTIONS:
- $50,000 wire transfer to an offshore account in the Cayman Islands on October 8, 1987
- Several cash withdrawals totaling $15,000 between September and October
- A check for $10,000 written to "V. Sinclair" dated October 12, 1987

3. LIFE INSURANCE POLICIES
Richard Thornton held two life insurance policies:
- Policy A: $1 million with Eleanor Thornton as beneficiary
- Policy B: $500,000 with James Thornton as beneficiary (recently modified from original $2 million)

4. CREDITORS
James Thornton has significant personal debt:
- Gambling debts totaling approximately $75,000
- Outstanding loans from two private lenders
- His credit cards were maxed out

NOTE: The check written to "V. Sinclair" may be connected to the "Victoria" mentioned in witness interviews. Further investigation recommended.
